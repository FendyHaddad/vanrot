# Code of Conduct

Vanrot should be a useful, respectful place to build a frontend framework.

This code of conduct applies to the repository, issues, pull requests, discussions, documentation, examples, and any project-maintained community space.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful, direct, and constructive.
- Keep technical disagreement focused on the idea, implementation, or evidence.
- Assume good intent, but correct problems clearly when they appear.
- Help new contributors understand the framework rules before judging their work.
- Credit other people's work and avoid misrepresenting authorship.
- Keep the project approachable for people with different levels of experience.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or sustained personal attacks.
- Discriminatory language or behavior.
- Sexualized comments, imagery, or attention.
- Publishing private information without consent.
- Deliberate disruption of issues, pull requests, reviews, or discussions.
- Bad-faith participation, including trolling, spam, or repeated refusal to follow maintainer direction.

## Reporting

If you see behavior that violates this code of conduct, report it to the maintainers.

Use a private channel when the report includes personal information, screenshots, private messages, or anything that could put someone at risk. If no private channel is available, open an issue with a short non-sensitive summary and ask for maintainer contact.

Reports should include:

- What happened.
- Where it happened.
- Who was involved.
- Any relevant links, screenshots, or timestamps.
- Whether immediate action is needed.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove content, close issues, reject pull requests, block accounts, or take other action needed to protect the project community.

Enforcement decisions should be proportional to the behavior, the impact, and any pattern of repeated conduct. Maintainers may also ask people to edit comments, clarify intent, apologize, or move a discussion back to technical substance.

## Scope

This code of conduct covers project spaces and behavior that affects the project community, including off-platform behavior when it directly harms contributors or maintainers.
